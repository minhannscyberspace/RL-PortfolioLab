Metrics glossary (how to read the tables)

  • final_equity: ending portfolio value. Higher is better.
  • total_reward: sum of per-step rewards ≈ return − costs − turnover penalty. Higher is better.
  • sharpe: mean(returns)/std(returns). Higher is better (NaN if std≈0).
  • max_drawdown: worst peak→trough equity drop fraction. Lower is better.
  • cvar_5: average of worst 5% returns. Less negative / higher is better.
  • avg_turnover: avg Σ|Δw| per step (trading aggressiveness). Lower is better.
  • walk-forward sharpe: distribution across out-of-sample folds; prefer higher median and better p10.
  • regimes: metrics computed within high-vol/low-vol/crash/calm slices.

Key takeaways

  • Best final equity: `equal_weight` (636401.6731233619)
  • Best Sharpe (in-sample backtest): `inverse_vol` (0.05064722299243669)

Methodology

  • Data: daily close prices → returns + rolling stats.
  • Action: continuous target weights per asset.
  • Turnover: Σ|w_t − w_{t−1}|.
  • Costs: transaction_cost_rate * turnover + slippage_rate * turnover (applied to equity).
  • Reward: return − cost − slippage − turnover_penalty (per-step).
  • Walk-forward: expanding train window; evaluate Sharpe distribution across OOS folds.
